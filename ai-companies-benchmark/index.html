The irony, stated plainly

Three of the ten companies we checked, all of them building products whose entire premise is an AI agent reading the web on a user's behalf, block that exact kind of automated, respectful fetch from reaching their own homepage. This isn't a security failure. It's a defensible business decision made by companies who, in the same breath, ask everyone else's website to be more open to exactly this kind of request. We aren't naming a villain here. We're naming the gap between what an industry sells and what its own front door does.

Methodology

One server-side fetch of each public homepage, a clearly identifying User-Agent, 9 weighted machine-readability checks (structured data, AI-crawler access, answer-shaped content, metadata, headings, sitemap, no-JS legibility, entity clarity, crawlability), public data only, one request per site, dated 2026-07-22. Sites returning a non-200 response (403, timeout, or similar) are reported as blocked, not scored as zero -- same honest-scope rule as every benchmark on this site.
