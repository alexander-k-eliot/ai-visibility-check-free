The State of AI Visibility: DTC & E-Commerce Brands 2026

We ran our AgentReady audit over 9 major direct-to-consumer e-commerce homepages on 2026-07-21. Shopping agents are the first place the agent economy touches real transactions, so this is the vertical where machine-readability turns directly into revenue.

77/100 average score. Median 80. Almost identical to last month's SaaS benchmark, but the failure pattern is completely different.

What almost everyone gets wrong

  • Structured data (schema.org) — only 56% pass. This is the one that should worry e-commerce brands most: Product schema (price, availability, reviews) is exactly what a shopping agent needs to compare and recommend, and almost half these brands don't publish it.
  • llms-full.txt and agents.md — 56% each. Better than the SaaS benchmark's 15-20%, but still a coin flip.

The interesting reversal: robots.txt and sitemap.xml are universal here (100% each, DTC brands clearly run competent basic SEO), but the AI-agent-specific layer, llms.txt, agents.md, and especially product-level structured data, lags well behind the SEO fundamentals. These brands optimized for Google. Almost none of them have optimized for an agent that shops on a customer's behalf.
