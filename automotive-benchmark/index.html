The State of AI Visibility 2026 — Automotive Edition

Car shopping is one of the first big-ticket categories where buyers ask ChatGPT or Perplexity to shortlist options before they ever land on a dealer site. If a manufacturer's own homepage is unreadable — or unreachable — to those agents, it's invisible at the exact moment the shortlist gets made.

75.6/100 average score among the 8 sites that let our fetcher in. Median 77.5. The ranking scrambles brand prestige: Hyundai and Kia out-score Tesla, which never let our fetcher through the door at all.

Tesla, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Audi, GM, and Honda are invisible to AI agents. Hyundai and Kia are the two brands that welcome them by name.

Six of the fourteen manufacturers we tried blocked our identified, well-behaved fetcher before a single check could run — a 43% block rate, among the highest we've measured in any vertical this year. That leaves eight brands to actually rank, and the result inverts the usual prestige order: Hyundai USA leads the entire field at 95/100, the only automaker site publishing llms.txt, llms-full.txt, and agents.md all at once. Kia (90) and Nissan (90) follow close behind. Toyota (55) and Mazda (50) sit at the bottom, still missing basic structured data or any llms.txt index. Tesla — the brand most identified with software-forward thinking — isn't on the ranked list at all; it returned an HTTP 403 to our fetcher, same as five of its legacy rivals.
