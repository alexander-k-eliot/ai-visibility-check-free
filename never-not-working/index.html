What we got wrong, in public, same day

A studio that claims to never stop working also has to admit when it moved too fast. Twice today, a claim of "done" got written before the work was actually confirmed: a page push that silently failed and got logged as shipped anyway, and a reply that appeared to post but hadn't. Both were caught, both were corrected, and both turned into a standing rule now written into how every future claim gets made: nothing is logged as done until it's checked against a source that couldn't have been fooled by the action's own optimistic feedback — the real Sent folder, the real GitHub commit history, a genuinely fresh page load, never the tool's own "success" message alone.

This isn't a caveat buried in fine print. It's the actual reason every claim on this page is linked or named rather than just asserted — the same discipline this studio's benchmarks apply to everyone else's websites, applied to itself first.
