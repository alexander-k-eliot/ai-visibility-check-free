The State of AI Visibility 2026 — Airline Edition

More travelers now ask an AI assistant to compare a fare, check a baggage policy, or find a flight-change rule than dig through an airline's own site. We ran our own AgentReady audit over 14 major airlines on 2026-07-22 to see how ready the industry is to be read and cited correctly by one at the moment a traveler asks.

43.6/100 average score among the 7 airlines that let our fetcher in. Median 45. The lowest average of any edition we have measured. The sharper finding is the other 7.

The real finding: half the industry's biggest names would not answer

7 of the 14 airlines we tried — United, Delta, American, JetBlue, Allegiant, Breeze, and Copa — returned no response to a single, clearly-identified, respectful server-side request (a mix of aborts, connection failures, and a 403). A closed door is not a low score, it is no data at all, so these 7 are excluded from the average rather than penalized, our standard honest-scope rule. But the exclusion is the story: three of the four largest US airlines by revenue, United, Delta, and American, plus JetBlue, are among the names blocking a plainly-identified automated request outright. Southwest, the one large-network carrier that did answer, scored lowest of the 7 scorable sites at 30. Of the 7 that answered, only one, Sun Country, a small budget carrier, publishes an llms.txt file, the machine-readable index an AI assistant looks for first.

Methodology

One server-side fetch of each public homepage, clear identifying User-Agent, 9 machine-readability checks (structured data, AI-crawler access, answer-shaped content, metadata, headings, sitemap, no-JS legibility, entity clarity, crawlability), weighted 0-100. Homepage-only snapshot, not a full-site audit, dated 2026-07-22. Public data only, no personal data, one request per site, and no attempt to work around any bot protection. This is a factual machine-readability scan, not a claim about any airline's safety record, on-time performance, or service quality. Scores change as sites change.
