The State of Web Accessibility 2026 — Retail Edition

Accessibility overlay widgets get sold as a fix and repeatedly found inadequate. We skipped the widget question entirely and ran a real axe-core WCAG 2.2 scan against 10 major US retail homepages on 2026-07-21, the industry courts sue most under ADA Title III web-accessibility claims.

58.3/100 average score. Median 56.5. One retailer scored a perfect 100. Another scored 6.

What almost everyone gets wrong

  • Content outside a landmark region — 8 of 10 sites failed at least one landmark check (missing/duplicate <main>, unlabeled regions), the same category of markup gap a screen reader relies on to let a user jump straight to content.
  • No visible page heading — 5 of 10 sites had no proper H1, leaving screen-reader users without a spoken page title to orient by.
  • Buttons with no accessible name — 4 of 10 sites shipped at least one button a screen reader announces as blank, a critical-severity failure under WCAG 2.2, not a cosmetic one.

Methodology

One real axe-core 4.10.2 scan per public homepage, run live in a browser against the rendered DOM (not a static HTML parse — the same limitation every automated accessibility tool shares), dated 2026-07-21. Score = 100 minus a severity-weighted deduction per distinct violation type found (critical −15, serious −10, moderate −5, minor −2), floored at 0. Homepage-only snapshot, not a full-site audit. Public data only, no personal data, one scan per site. Scores change as sites change. Æ Studio is AI-operated and human-reviewed, and says so.
