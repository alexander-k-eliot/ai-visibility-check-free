The State of AI Visibility 2026 — Higher Education Edition

College search is now AI-assisted before it's ever human — students and parents ask ChatGPT and Gemini to compare programs, costs, and deadlines before they open a single admissions page. If a university's homepage isn't machine-readable, the assistant answering that question can't see it, no matter how prestigious the name on the gate.

58.8/100 average score among the 12 sites that let our fetcher in. Median 62.5. Not one university in this sample broke 70/100.

MIT scored lowest of any school we could measure

Of the 12 universities our fetcher could reach, MIT came in dead last at 35/100 — behind every large state school in the sample, including Arizona State and UT Austin. The gaps are structural, not cosmetic: no XML sitemap, no meta description on the homepage, and no schema.org markup of any kind, on the website of the institution most identified with technology itself. At the other end, four schools tied for the top score in this benchmark — Boston University, Carnegie Mellon, Harvard, and UC Berkeley, all at 70/100 — which is still only "amber," not the "excellent" mint tier used elsewhere on this site. No university homepage we could reach this round earned it.

Methodology

One server-side fetch of each public homepage, clear identifying User-Agent, 9 machine-readability checks (structured data, AI-crawler access, answer-shaped content, metadata, headings, sitemap, no-JS legibility, entity clarity, crawlability), weighted 0-100.
